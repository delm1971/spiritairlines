How to find the lowest real price

Cheap airfare is not only about the base ticket price. Always compare the full trip cost before booking.

🧳

Compare bag fees

A low base fare can become expensive once carry-on, checked bag, seat, and boarding fees are added.

📅

Try flexible dates

Flying one day earlier or later can often reveal cheaper fares, especially around weekends and holidays.

🛫

Check nearby airports

Former Spirit routes may have affordable options from nearby airports or alternative carriers.

Do not book only by the headline fare.

Before you purchase, compare refund rules, schedule changes, carry-on allowance, seat fees, airport location, and the total price for every traveler.

Best alternatives to check first

Use this checklist when replacing a canceled, unavailable, or expensive flight.

✓
Low-cost carriers: Compare airlines such as Frontier, Breeze, Southwest, JetBlue, Allegiant, Avelo, and other carriers serving your route.
✓
Legacy airlines: Delta, American, United, and Alaska may be competitive once bags and seat fees are included.
✓
Bundle savings: If you need a hotel or rental car, package pricing may lower your overall trip cost.
✓
Refund protection: Review cancellation rules before booking, especially for last-minute replacement flights.

Quick comparison checklist

What to compare Why it matters Action
Base fare The advertised price may not include bags, seats, or extras. Compare 2–3 booking sites.
Carry-on and checked bags Bag fees can change which flight is truly cheapest. Add baggage before comparing totals.
Departure airport Nearby airports may have lower fares or better replacement routes. Search surrounding airports.
Schedule and layovers A cheaper fare may involve long layovers or risky connections. Balance price with travel time.
